Music

After making one song in 2014 I sat on it for a year before cranking out two more in 2015. This kickstarted the Chip Party project, which ended in 2016 when I decided to ditch chiptune to experiment with other things.

Illusions of Fortune (Chip Party):


2017 was the year of P.I.A.N.O., a set of three volumes consisting of entirely solo piano music.

Powder (P.I.A.N.O.: Vol 2):


In 2018 I released a remastered collection of the entire P.I.A.N.O. library plus an EP called VI alongside four previously unreleased tracks titled R.E.P.I.A.N.O.M.I.X (a staggering 32 songs!).

Powder (R.E.P.I.A.N.O.M.I.X):


2020 was the year of more experimentation, breaking from the piano chains to start adding more instruments. This resulted in sys(FAIL and oscillations, two very unique EP's that would lay out the groundwork for how my music would sound going forward.

oscillations (oscillations):


After all the time put into the aforementioned EPs I wanted to create a "best of" album that would reflect the range of music I've made. This became TemApex, though I just call it the blue album. It doesn't contain motifs unlike a lot of my older works and the production quality is a lot higher, so it's a good starting point if you're a new listener.

System Failure (TemApex):


After the blue album came Keep On Going - a single that I still hold as being one of my best works - and Aftermath, an EP made from the remnants of a cancelled album.

Keep On Going:


Given my history it might sound like I know what I'm doing, but truthfully I don't. I have had no musical training or education and just work off vibes alone, for better or worse.


Some of my personal favourite songs are System Failure, Breaking & Entering, Seasons, Powder, and Perfect Resolution.
